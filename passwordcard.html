Password Card Generator

This application is a JavaScript standalone application to generate password cards. You can put it on your own webserver or use it from your harddrive.

The cards print exactly in credit card size and can be used to generate passwords. The advantage: the passwords are strictly separated from your electronic devices.

The idea is from the german magazine c't, issue 18/2014, p. 92. https://www.heise.de/ct/ausgabe/2014-18-Werkzeuge-gegen-das-Passwort-Chaos-2283736.html

The generated cards look like the one above. Because they are generated in real time and do not persist on computing devices (if you exercise effective printer management hygene), they can be employed in ways that resist broad categories of attack. This approach has no magic, though, so risk analysis of your password lifecycle practices is still an important regular exercise.

How to use the card

Assume you have an account at example.com. You take the first four letters of the domain name ("exam") and lookup the three letter combinations in the first row of the matching columns, that is s%5 for the "e", v3O for the "x", 8v( for the "a" and finally c5L for the "m". Your first password for your account at example.com is s%5v3O8v(c5L.

When it is time to change your password you generate a new one using the second row (and then the next, and so on). After nine passwords, maybe you should generate a new card and migrate to that over time(?). Again, this is not perfect. It is a time-tested option that has a given attack surface -- one that is different from many other password management options
